My Journey So Far

Authored by Zyre Jerence L. Sison

Childhood Years

1Zyre Jerence L. Sison spent his early years in an average, bustling neighborhood in the Philippines, growing up in a typical middle-class family. 2His home was often filled with the lively noise of relatives and the smell of home-cooked meals, usually adobo or sinigang, an important part of his childhood. 3He remembers the intense heat of summer days, spent playing patintero or tagu-taguan with the neighborhood kids until the streetlights came on. 4Like many Filipino children, his first introduction to technology was often at the local computer shop, where he spent his tiny allowance on an hour of gaming. 5The concept of bayanihan was instilled in him early, watching neighbors help each other during small community cleanups or family events. 6His mother taught him the value of thriftiness and making the most of limited resources, often stretching a small budget successfully. 7 His father emphasizes the importance of education as the only path toward a better future. Zyre was an average student, not the valedictorian, but diligent enough to always pass his classes and maintain good grades. 8His favorite activity was talking about video games or playing video games with classmates and friends. 9He often had to share resources, whether it was a small bedroom with a sibling or a single school textbook among several classmates. 10The yearly tradition of preparing for the Flores de Mayo or Simbang Gabi always brought a sense of cultural pride and community spirit. 11He learned early on how to use public transportation, mastering the complicated routes of the jeepney to run errands for his parents. 12A memorable moment was receiving his first second-hand smart phone, which felt like the height of luxury and technological advancement. 13His childhood was characterized by resilience, resourcefulness, and the warmth of a Filipino family structure. 14Even simple moments, like sharing a cold glass of RC cola, felt significant and deeply cherished. 15He learned to appreciate the smaller joys in life, knowing that big luxuries were often out of reach. 16The noise and energy of his environment, from tricycle engines to vendor calls, became the soundtrack of his childhood years. 17The streets I played on became symbols of both happiness and struggle. 18This average, normal Philippine childhood laid a practical, grounded foundation for his future. 19His childhood shaped the person he carries today and built the strength to continue on. 20Looking back, those fun and shiny years form a story that will always stick to him.

Teenage Years

1Entering high school marked the phase where Zyre became increasingly aware of the socio-economic gap surrounding him, giving him a quiet determination to succeed. 2His teenage years involved the familiar balancing act of academics, limited social life, and contributing to household responsibilities. 3He started to spend more time experimenting with computers, often visiting computer shops not just for games but to explore basic programming tutorials online. 4His group of friends was a mix of practical jokers and serious students, united by shared anxieties over college entrance exams and the latest trends. 5Financial problems meant he couldn't always join school outings or purchase the newest gadgets, which gave creativity in finding free and affordable entertainment. 6He joined the school's computer club, realizing his genuine interest in solving technical problems and finding logic in digital systems. 7His exposure to the digital world showed the gap between local technology and global standards, fueling his desire to learn more. 8He took on small side jobs, such as helping neighbors fix basic computer issues, using his growing knowledge to earn a little extra pocket money. 9He learned to maximize the efficiency of older computer hardware, a practical skill born out of necessity rather than choice. 10Peer pressure was present, but the constant reminder of his parents' sacrifices kept him focused on his studies and long-term goals. 11The intense preparation for the university entrance tests became the central focus of his junior and senior years. 12He saw his college degree, especially in a growing field like IT, as the family's best bet towards mobility and stability. 13He became skilled at time management, juggling homework, family duties, and his technical tinkering late into the night. 14He navigated the dynamics of high school relationships, experiencing the typical ups and downs of young adult emotional growth. 15He learned the value of network connectivity, both digital and personal, understanding that connections could open doors. 16His dedication to learning the technical ropes proved that an average student could find excellence through specialized interest. 17He faced the common pressure to choose a stable, high-demand career, which firmly cemented his decision to pursue Information Technology. 18Pandemic came and crushed all of his efforts and determination. 19His mind became sluggish, lazy, and hopeless which made him lose all of the skill he worked hard for. 20As graduation came, he found himself trying to rebuild himself with the little hope and determination he has.

College Years

1As the time to start college came, Zyre's journey to Information Technology porgram at the University of the East began with high hopes but quickly introduced him to the exhausting demands of a technical education. 2The shift from high school to the fast-paced college environment, particularly in a demanding field, required immediate adaptation and focus. 3His first year was a messy foundation, grappling with core subjects like computer programming and others, which often felt like learning a completely new language. 4He quickly understood that success in IT relied heavily on consistent practice and not just repetitive memorization for exams. 5The academic pressure was compounded by the typical financial realities, necessitating careful budgeting for transportation and food near the busy campus. 6Commuting to and from UE through the heavy rush hours became an unavoidable part of his daily routine, teaching him patience and time management. 7They supported me during stressful moments and shared lessons that helped me grow. 8He found similarities with his classmates, often relying on group study sessions to learn more about programming concepts like flowcharts and basic python syntax. 9As a second-year student now, the curriculum has intensified, requiring him to tackle more abstract and crucial courses like Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) and Structured Query Language (SQL). 10These courses proved to be the most challenging yet, demanding creative problem-solving and systematic debugging skills. 11He is learning to utilize the university's computer labs, even if the equipment isn't the latest, prioritizing access and practice time over aesthetics. There are days when college is exhausting, but there are also days filled with opportunities and fun. 12Zyre has started to explore the various branches of IT, slowly determining whether he leans toward software development, network administration, or cybersecurity. 13He actively participates in coding exercises online, sharing and learning with other growing programmers like him. 14He has learned the critical importance of attending laboratory sessions, knowing that hands-on application is far more valuable than simply reading the theory. 15The constant fear of failing a major programming course serves as a powerful motivator, pushing him to spend extra hours coding and reviewing technical notes. 16He utilizes free online platforms like YouTube tutorials and developer forums to supplement the classroom lectures, adopting a self-directed learning approach. 17The pursuit of his degree is viewed as an investment, not just by him, but by his family, making every passed subject feel like a collective victory. 18He is mastering the critical skill of efficiently managing multiple project deadlines and study schedules, a necessary trait for any future IT professional. 19His experience at UE is currently shaping him into a practical, resilient technical student ready to face the next few years. 20This challenging and rewarding journey is building the core knowledge he needs to secure a stable and promising career in the Philippine tech industry.
